# Rise and Fall of Kingfisher Airlines – Business Case Study Dashboard

## Project Overview
This mini project analyzes the business failure of Kingfisher Airlines through a data-driven dashboard approach.  
The objective of this case study is to understand the major financial and operational reasons behind the airline’s downfall using key business metrics and visual storytelling.

The dashboard highlights the company’s timeline, debt burden, loss trends, market position, and failure drivers.

## Key KPIs Covered
- Total Employees: 5696
- Loan Outstanding: ₹4,000 Cr
- Destinations Covered: 128
- Consecutive Loss Years: 3
- Total Loss: ₹7,000 Cr

---

## Business Insights
### 1. Financial Crisis
The company accumulated a total loss of ₹7,000 Cr while carrying an outstanding debt of ₹4,000 Cr.

### 2. Timeline Analysis
The timeline visual explains the rise and fall of the airline from launch to operational shutdown.

### 3. Market Position
The dashboard shows the decline in market performance and the final drop in 2012.

### 4. Root Cause Analysis
Major reasons identified:
- High operating cost
- Fuel cost pressure
- Heavy debt
- Air Deccan acquisition impact
- Employee cost burden

---

## Business Recommendation
This case study highlights the importance of:
- sustainable debt management
- cost optimization
- operational efficiency
- strategic expansion decisions

---

## Conclusion
Kingfisher Airlines serves as a strong business case study on how financial mismanagement, high debt, and operational inefficiencies can lead to business failure despite strong brand presence.
